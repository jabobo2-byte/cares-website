Know the Law

  • Seatbelts, always. Tennessee is a primary seatbelt state — every driver and front-seat passenger must buckle up, and anyone under 17 must buckle up in the back seat too.
  • No texting, ever. Reading or sending texts while driving is illegal for all drivers in Tennessee.
  • No phone at all for permit & intermediate license holders — not even hands-free — until you're 18.
  • Learner's permit curfew: no driving between 10 p.m. and 6 a.m., and a licensed adult (21+) must ride in the front seat.
  • Move over for stopped emergency vehicles — change lanes or slow way down.
